Welcome

Where the Oceans Embrace

Kanyakumari (officially Kanniyakumari, formerly Cape Comorin) is the southernmost town of mainland India, nestled in Tamil Nadu at the meeting point of the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and the Indian Ocean — a phenomenon known as Triveni Sangam.

It is the only place on Earth where you can witness both sunrise and sunset from the same vantage point. On full moon nights (especially Chithra Pournami), the sun and moon appear facing each other on the same horizon — a celestial spectacle found nowhere else.

The Past

A Journey Through Time

From the dawn of civilization to the present day, Kanyakumari's story spans four millennia.

~2000 BC

Neolithic Civilization

Earliest evidence of human settlement with Neolithic Celts dating to 1500-1000 BC discovered in the region.

~276 BC

Mentioned by Ptolemy

The Greek scholar mentions "Komari" as a harbor in the Pandian Kingdom. Also referenced by Marco Polo and in Sangam-era Tamil literature.

9th-18th Century

Dynastic Rule

Ruled by Cheras, Cholas, Pandyas, Ays, Nayaks, and the Venad/Travancore kingdom. The Battle of Colachel took place here.

1656

Cape Comorin

Dutch East India Company shortened "Kanya Kumari" to "Comorin." During British rule, it became "Cape Comorin."

1956

Merger with Tamil Nadu

After a historic agitation led by Marshal A. Nesamony, four Tamil-speaking taluks merged with Madras State (now Tamil Nadu).

2016

Renamed Kanniyakumari

The Government of India officially renamed Cape Comorin to Kanniyakumari, restoring its ancient Tamil name.

Iconic Attractions

From ancient temples to modern marvels, Kanyakumari offers an unforgettable tapestry of experiences.

Vivekananda Rock Memorial

Built in 1970 on a rocky islet 500m offshore, marking the spot where Swami Vivekananda attained enlightenment in 1892. Accessible by ferry from 8 AM to 4 PM daily.

Thiruvalluvar Statue

A 133ft (41m) stone colossus of the Tamil poet-philosopher weighing 7,000 tons. The 38ft pedestal represents virtue, and the 133ft total height represents the 133 chapters of the Thirukkural.

Bhagavathy Amman Temple

A 3,000-year-old Shakti Peetha dedicated to Goddess Kanya Kumari. The idol's ruby nose ring was so bright it was once mistaken for a lighthouse by sailors.

Sunset Point & Pier

One of the most iconic spots in Kanyakumari, offering breathtaking sunset views over the confluence of three oceans. A must-visit for every traveler.

Vattakottai Fort

An 18th-century circular granite fort built by Travancore, overseen by Dutch commander Eustachius De Lannoy. Overlooks both the Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea.

Sunrise at Kanyakumari

The only place in India where you can watch the sunrise over the Bay of Bengal and sunset over the Arabian Sea from the exact same location.

Coastal Beauty

Pristine Beaches

With 71.5 km of coastline washed by three oceans, Kanyakumari's beaches are among India's most spectacular.

Kanyakumari Beach

The main beach at the tip of the peninsula — the only place in India where you can watch sunrise over the Bay of Bengal and sunset over the Arabian Sea from the same spot.

Sothavilai (Chothavilai) Beach

A serene stretch of golden sand, famous for spectacular sunsets. The name means "golden beach" in Tamil, and it lives up to its name.

Sanguthurai Beach

Adorned with golden sands and glittering waters, this beach offers a tranquil escape surrounded by swaying palms and stunning coastal views.

Wild Landscapes

Forests & Mountains

Beyond the coastline, Kanyakumari is blessed with the majestic Western Ghats — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's eight most biodiverse regions. The mountains rise up to 1,829 meters and are blanketed by 17 distinct forest types.

Mountains of Kanyakumari

The southernmost ranges of the Western Ghats rise dramatically from the coastal plains. These mountains form the boundary between Tamil Nadu and Kerala and are home to rich biodiversity.

Kalikesam Waterfalls

Tucked deep inside the Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary, Kalikesam is a pristine forest area with cascading waterfalls. The name means "Kali's abode" and the area is inhabited by the indigenous Kani tribe.

Mukkadal & Kodayar

The upper reaches of Kanyakumari district feature lush shola forests, grassy downs, and reservoirs like Mukkadal and Kodayar — feeding seven rivers that originate in these mountains.

Western Ghats Connection

The Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary forms the southernmost part of the Agasthiyamalai range in the Western Ghats. This region is recognized as a global biodiversity hotspot with 62% of the Western Ghats' endemic plant species found here. The sanctuary connects with Kalakkad-Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve (KMTR) and Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary (Kerala), forming a vital wildlife corridor spanning over 800 km2.

Seven rivers originate from these forests — including the Thamirabarani and Pahrali — sustaining millions of people downstream. The elevation ranges from 50m at the coast to 1,829m in the montane shola forests, creating a remarkable diversity of habitats in a compact area.

Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary

Declared a protected area in February 2008, this 402.39 km2 sanctuary forms the southernmost ranges of the Agasthiyamalai forest tract. It is a designated tiger habitat and connects with Kalakkad-Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve (KMTR) and Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary (Kerala) to form the largest protected forest complex in southern India. Several new species of plants, amphibians, and insects discovered here are found nowhere else on Earth.

Forest Types (17)

Evergreen Forests

Southern Hilltop Tropical Evergreen, West Coast Tropical Evergreen, and Tirunelveli Semi-Evergreen — dense, multi-layered canopies at various elevations.

Deciduous Forests

Moist & Dry Teak Forests, Southern Moist & Dry Mixed Deciduous — seasonal forests with valuable timber species like teak and Terminalia.

Thorn & Scrub

Carnatic Umbrella Thorn, Southern Thorn Forests, and Thorn Scrub — arid-adapted vegetation with Acacia and Euphorbia species.

Grasslands & Bamboo

Southern Montane Wet Grasslands (shola-grassland mosaic), Wet Bamboo Brakes — unique high-altitude and riparian ecosystems.

Wildlife

Flavours

Coastal Cuisine

Kanyakumari's cuisine is a delicious marriage of Tamil and Malayali culinary traditions, with seafood taking center stage. The town is a major fishery base in South India, ensuring the freshest catch daily.

  • Nandu Masala — Spicy crab preparation, a local signature dish
  • Meen Kuzhambu — Traditional Tamil fish curry
  • Kottu — Minced fried paratha with vegetables, egg, or meat
  • Appam — Rice and coconut pancake (Kerala influence)
  • Avial & Theeyal — Coconut-based vegetable curries
  • Paazha Sharbat — Unique mixed fruit juice specialty
  • Tapioca with Fish — Regional non-vegetarian staple
Did You Know?

Unique & Fascinating

Kanyakumari is full of surprises. Here are some of the most remarkable facts about this extraordinary land.

Three Oceans Meet

Kanyakumari is the only place in India where the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean converge at one point — Triveni Sangam.

Sunrise & Sunset

The only place on Earth where you can watch both sunrise and sunset from the exact same location.

Lighthouse Nose Ring

The goddess idol in Kumari Amman Temple has a ruby nose ring so bright that ships mistook it for a lighthouse and crashed. The sea-facing gate stays closed except for 5 days a year.

Rice Stone Legend

The small rice-like stones on the shore are believed to be uncooked wedding rice from the failed marriage of Goddess Kanya Devi.

Kashmir to Kanyakumari

The famous phrase describing India's length originates here. Before partition, it was 'Khyber se Kanyakumari'.

Richest District

Kanyakumari district has the highest per capita income and HDI in all of Tamil Nadu, and the second-highest population density.

Rich Christian Heritage

At 61% Christian, Kanyakumari is unique in Tamil Nadu as a Christian-majority town, with a strong history of missionary influence.

Chithra Pournami

On full moon nights in April-May, the sun and moon face each other on the same horizon — a rare astronomical spectacle unique to this latitude.
